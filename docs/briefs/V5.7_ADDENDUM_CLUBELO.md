## Part 2.6 — Bounded rating carry-forward (a deliberate switch, not an automatism)

**The physical fact that makes this sound:** a clubelo rating is a step
function — it changes only when a club plays. During a genuine off-season a
rating from 3 July *is* the rating on 23 July; clubelo has merely not extended
the row. Carrying it forward is therefore not an estimate.

**The blind spot that bounds it:** the pipeline sees only BL1/BL2 fixtures. It
cannot see DFB-Pokal or European qualifying, and European qualifying runs in
July. A long carry-forward is therefore *not* safe, and the rule must expire.

Implement as an explicit, time-boxed CLI switch — **fail-closed stays the
default**:

```
node pipeline/src/cli.mjs --data-dir data --carry-forward-until=YYYY-MM-DD
```

Contract:

1. **Off by default.** Without the flag, behaviour is exactly as today: an
   unresolved club fails the job and blocks the commit (§5.2, unchanged).
2. **Per-club, not global.** Only clubs absent from the daily snapshot are
   carried forward. A club that *is* present always uses its live value.
3. **Preconditions, all required.** A club may be carried forward only if:
   - the requested date is on or before `--carry-forward-until`;
   - the club has **no fixture known to the pipeline** with a kickoff between
     its snapshot's `effectiveAt` and the requested date;
   - a prior snapshot for that club exists in the archive.
   Any precondition failing ⇒ the club is unresolved ⇒ the job fails closed.
4. **Hard ceiling: 42 days** between `effectiveAt` and the requested date,
   regardless of the flag. Beyond that the value is refused even with the
   switch set. The flag can shorten this, never extend it.
5. **`effectiveAt` is never rewritten.** The carried entry keeps the real date
   of the rating it came from and adds `provenance: "carried-forward"`
   alongside the existing `contemporaneous` / `backfilled` values of §5.3.
   Model-quality evaluations must not silently pool it with the others — the
   same rule §5.3 already applies to backfilled ratings.
6. **Visible in the app, per club.** Any club running on a carried rating is
   marked in the table and on its Teams page: „Rating vom 3. Juli — clubelo
   führt diesen Klub derzeit nicht fort." The header carries one summary line
   while any carried rating is in use. This is a §8 honesty requirement, not a
   nicety: a forecast partly built on stale inputs must say so.
7. **Self-clearing.** The moment clubelo lists a club again, its live value is
   used and the marker disappears — no manual reset, no migration.
8. **Logged loudly.** Every carried club is named in the workflow log with its
   `effectiveAt` and its age in days, so the Actions notification stays a
   truthful record of what was committed.

**Tests:** a club present in the snapshot is never carried; a club absent
without the flag still fails the job; a club with an intervening known fixture
fails even with the flag; the 42-day ceiling refuses regardless of the flag;
a carried entry keeps its original `effectiveAt` and carries the new
provenance; the marker appears and disappears with the underlying state.

## Part 2.7 — The daily CSV must be verified against the date requested

While clubelo serves cached pages, a response can be structurally valid
(correct header, ~600 rows) and still describe **a different day**. The current
guards would not notice.

Add to `fetchDailySnapshot`: after parsing, assert that the requested date
falls within `[From, To]` for a **substantial majority of rows** (threshold:
≥ 90 %). If fewer rows cover it, throw `RatingSourceError` naming the requested
date and the date range the response actually describes — a stale cache is a
source failure, not data.

This check is deliberately row-based rather than all-or-nothing: individual
clubs legitimately fall outside the range (exactly the four we have now), so a
strict «every row must cover the date» rule would fail permanently. The
majority test distinguishes «a few clubs are stuck» from «this CSV is from
another day».

**Tests:** a snapshot whose rows cover the requested date passes; a snapshot
shifted by weeks throws; a snapshot where only a handful of clubs fall outside
the range still passes and those clubs surface as unresolved (feeding 2.6).
